Why We Should Celebrate Diversity & Difference

The Importance Of Diversity

Diversity within our society is vital as it enables us to gather multiple perspectives on differing topics and aids us in growing a love and respect for other cultures. Diversity aids us in recognizing the beauty of individualism and uniqueness within our society, expelling personal bias and our negative stereotypes.

The saying 'You cannot attain diversity of thought without diversity of people' highlights the signficiance of difference within our modern communities. Exhibiting that diveristy enables us to innovate with the inclusion of new ideas from a new perspective. Without diversity our world would be like a book with only the same word repeating within it.


Positive Of Diveristy

Some examples for the plethora of positives of diversity given by Partnership International encompass:

  • Respect for the racial differences
  • Recognition of the abundant diversity of cultures
  • Acknowledging the uniqueness of cultural expressions and importance of their contributions
  • Encouraging the contribution of diverse groups
  • Empowering people to strengthen themselves by critically analysing their actions and their own biases
  • Celebrating the differences in race in order to spark unity through diversity
  • Gaining new concepts and perspectives on current ideas through the difference in thought, lifestyle, upbringing, etc.

Contributions of Races

Each race within the scope of our world has offered a inexplicable amount of contributions allowing us to advance throughout modern day and become stronger as humans. Diversity strenghtens current concepts, provides a fresh and different perspective to existing notions and innovate our current society.

Contributions from all races include:


  • African contributions, such as a heavy impact on civil rights and the creation of the Three-Signal Traffic light

  • Middle Eastern involvements, incorporating the concept of irrational numbers, founding analytical geometry and establishing algebra and trigonometry

  • Asian particpation in our society, including the innovations of papermaking, printing, gunpowder and the compass

  • European contributions, encompassing the revolutionising concepts of the submarine, the celsius thermometer and the space suit

  • Indian input, including the invention of the the number 'Zero' and constructing the world's first university

  • North American involvments, such as the generation of the electric lightbulb and the first suspension bridge

  • South American participation, encompassing the inventions of the colour TV and the development of the artificial heart

  • Australian contributions, involving the creations of the electronic pacemaker and the cochlear implant

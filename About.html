About The Impacts Of Racial Discrimination

Definition

Racial Discrimination is described as the situation when an individual is treated less favourably, or not given the same opportunities, as others in a similar situation,
due to their race.


Impacts

Racial Discrimination is an incredibly harmful act, causing immense impacts on the mental health and wellbeing of individuals. Racially discriminating against someone causes them to feel as though they do not belong within our society, to feel as though they are an outcast who should not exist in a certain community. Demonstrated in the statistics below.



Statistics

In a survey about racial discrimination provided to Australian members of Victoria, these results were found:

  • 55% were called racist names, teased or heard jokes or comments that relied on stereotypes

  • 49% were sworn at, verbally abused or subjected to offensive gestures because of their race.

  • 44% were ignored, treated with suspicion or treated rudely because of their race.

  • 44% were told they were less intelligent than or inferior to people from other races.

  • 44% were told that they did not belong because of their race.

  • 38% were left out or avoided because of their race.

  • 32% were spat at, had an object thrown at them, were hit or threatened to be hit because of their race.

  • 26% had their property vandalised because of their race.
